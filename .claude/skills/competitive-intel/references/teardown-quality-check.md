# Teardown Quality Check

Run after completing all 4 steps of Mode 1 (Teardown). Score each dimension before delivering output.

---

## Quality Gate (score before delivering)

| Check | Pass condition | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Data sources** | ≥ 3 sources per competitor (website + reviews + jobs minimum) | ✅ / ❌ |
| **Review data** | ≥ 10 customer reviews analyzed per competitor | ✅ / ❌ |
| **Pricing confirmed** | Pricing data from actual pricing page (not guessed) | ✅ / ❌ |
| **All 12 dimensions scored** | Every row in the rubric has a score + evidence note | ✅ / ❌ |
| **Positioning map populated** | At least 3 entities placed on the 2x2 | ✅ / ❌ |
| **SWOT complete** | ≥ 3 bullets per quadrant, each anchored to data | ✅ / ❌ |
| **Action items tiered** | Quick wins / Medium / Strategic separated | ✅ / ❌ |

**Pass threshold:** All 7 checks must pass before delivering the teardown.
If any check fails: note what's missing and either gather the data or explicitly flag "data unavailable."

---

## Common Gaps to Watch For

**Pricing page changes frequently** — confirm data is < 30 days old.

**Review sentiment bias** — G2/Capterra skews toward happy customers. Balance with Reddit, Twitter, and churned customer signals.

**Job posting lag** — job postings reflect plans from 60–90 days ago. Mark signals as "pending" not "confirmed."

**Competitor messaging vs. reality** — their homepage copy is marketing. Their 1-star reviews are truth. Weight accordingly.

**Missing = competitive advantage** — if a competitor has no reviews, no pricing, no case studies: that's a weakness in itself (low traction or opaque). Note it as a finding.

---

## Scoring Calibration

Before finalizing scores, run this sanity check:

- Does your own product score honestly? (Overscoring yourself kills credibility)
- Is there at least 1 dimension where each competitor scores higher than you? (If not, you're probably not being honest)
- Are your scores defensible with evidence? (Could you explain every score in a sales call?)

**Conservative scoring rule:** When unsure between two score levels, use the lower one.
A score you can defend beats a higher score you have to walk back.
