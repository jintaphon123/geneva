# Pitch Deck Template

Slide-by-slide guide. Adapt to context: KU Senior Project pitch ≠ VC seed pitch.
Read context first — then fill in from Bond's actual situation.

---

## Slide 1 — Cover

```
[Company/Project Name]
[One-line tagline — what you do for whom]
[Bond's name] | [Date] | [Context: KU Senior Project / Seed Pitch / Demo Day]
```

Rule: No logos, no clutter. Name + tagline + who's presenting + when.

---

## Slide 2 — Problem

**Structure:**
- Who has this problem? (specific person, not "everyone")
- What does the problem look like day-to-day? (make it vivid)
- How painful / how often? (quantify if possible)
- Why haven't existing solutions fixed it? (the gap)

**Bond's Second Brain problem:**
> Non-technical people can't build persistent AI memory. Every ChatGPT session starts from zero. Knowledge doesn't compound. The more you use AI, the less it knows about you.

**Rule:** Show the pain before showing the solution. If the problem doesn't land, the solution doesn't matter.

---

## Slide 3 — Solution

**Structure:**
- What you built (one sentence)
- How it works (show, don't tell — screenshot or 3-step diagram)
- The key insight (what makes this approach different)

**Rule:** If you need more than 3 sentences to explain it, the solution isn't clear enough yet.

---

## Slide 4 — Demo / Product

Show the product. If no live demo: screenshots with annotations.
If prototype only: be honest — "This is our prototype. Here's what the production version will do."

**Rule:** Never describe what the product does when you can show it.

---

## Slide 5 — Traction

Show the best honest number you have. No traction = say so, but explain the evidence of demand.

| Stage | What to show |
|---|---|
| Idea | "10 people said they'd pay for this" + quotes |
| Prototype | "5 users tested it, 4 came back without prompting" |
| MVP | "X users, Y% retention, Z feedback quotes" |
| Revenue | ARR, MoM growth, churn rate |

**Rule:** Never inflate numbers. Investors talk to each other. One lie = game over.

---

## Slide 6 — Market Size

**TAM / SAM / SOM** — be realistic, not astronomically large:
- **TAM:** Total market if you had 100% (often global or industry-wide)
- **SAM:** Serviceable segment you can realistically reach
- **SOM:** What you'll realistically capture in 3–5 years

**Rule:** A ฿100B TAM claim with no path to it is worse than a ฿10B TAM with a clear entry point.

---

## Slide 7 — Business Model

Answer: how do you make money?

```
Revenue model: [Subscription / Usage / Freemium+Pro / B2B SaaS]
Price point: [฿X/month per user] or [฿X/month per team]
Unit economics: [CAC target] / [LTV target] / [Payback period]
```

If pre-revenue: show the model + comparable benchmarks.

---

## Slide 8 — Competitive Landscape

2x2 map — place yourself in white space.
Axes must show YOUR differentiation (not generic "price vs quality").

```
         High [Axis Y]
              │
    [Comp A]  │        [US]
              │
──────────────┼──────────────── High [Axis X]
              │
    [Comp B]  │   [Comp C]
              │
         Low [Axis Y]
```

Brief table below: 3–5 competitors × 3–5 key dimensions. ✅/❌ per cell.

---

## Slide 9 — Team

For Bond at KU stage:
- Photo + name + role + most relevant credential
- Why are YOU the right person to build this?
- What have you already built / shipped / learned?

**Rule:** "We're passionate" is not a credential. Show relevant experience or acknowledge the gap honestly.

---

## Slide 10 — Roadmap

Next 12–18 months in 3 phases:

```
Phase 1 (Now → Month 3): [Milestone — e.g., "KU team formed, MVP with 50 users"]
Phase 2 (Month 3 → 9): [Milestone — e.g., "Commercial launch, 500 users, first revenue"]
Phase 3 (Month 9 → 18): [Milestone — e.g., "Web UI, 2000 users, ฿XXk ARR"]
```

---

## Slide 11 — The Ask

Be specific. Vague asks get vague responses.

```
We are raising: [฿X million / $X seed round]
For: [specific use of funds — engineering X months, marketing Y, operations Z]
This gets us to: [specific milestone — e.g., PMF with 1000 users / ฿Xm ARR]
```

For KU Senior Project pitch: replace money with resources/mentorship/team ask.
